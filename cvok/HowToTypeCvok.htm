How To Type Colville-Okanagan

This package installs the cvok keyboard on your computer. It also installs the Charis SIL font, a font that has all the characters needed for Colville-Okanagan. A Windows font that has almost all the needed characters is Lucida Sans Unicode. Select one of these fonts when entering data in your word processor.

This page explains how to:
Type a reassigned key
Restore a reassigned key
Type stressed vowels
Type glottalized or laryngealized consonants
Type • (bullet), ḥ (sub-dot h), and x̌ (x-wedge)
Type rounded consonants
Type rounded and glottalized consonants

When the OkanaganU keyboard is selected, the keys shown in the grey row are reassigned to Okanagan letter symbols as shown in the white row:

 
4678v|
ƛ̓ʕʔɣəɬ

The following keys are also reassigned to punctuation or other non-letter symbols as follows:
QW:=_
√ʷˑ‿
Note: the short double hyphen shows correctly in the Charis SIL font, but may not in other Unicode fonts.

To restore any of the reassigned keys without exiting the OkanaganU keyboard, type a zero (0) followed by the wanted key:
00040607080|0Q0W0:0=0_
04678|QW:=_

To obtain an accented vowel, type the vowel as shown in the gray row followed by / or \ to get the acute or grave vowel shown in the white row:  
aeiou
áéíóú/
àèìòù\
Note: e is not an Okanagan vowel. The symbols are provided for convenience.

Glottalized or laryngealized consonant are obtained typing the consonant key followed by the apostrophe:
ptckqmnlry8w6
p̓t̓c̓k̓q̓m̓n̓l̕r̓y̓ɣ̓w̓ʕ̓'

A bullet (•) is obtained by typing H (capital H) followed by : (colon):
H:
•

ḥ is obtained by typing . (period) followed by the letter as shown:
.h

x̌ is obtained by typing H (capital H) followed by x
Hx

Rounded consonants are obtained typing the consonant key followed by W (capital W):
kqx.x6
kʷqʷxʷx̌ʷʕʷW

Glottalized and rounded consonants are obtained typing the consonant followed by the apostrophe and the capital W:
k'Wq'W6'W
k̓ʷq̓ʷʕ̓ʷ

Note: When the cursor is over any of the symbols (or symbol sequences) in the white rows, their Unicode values are displayed.
